Stepping into the world of Internship

Week One

Before the official start of our internship, we had to comply with all the necessary requirements for the application. My group and I decided to apply at the Department of Education – Schools Division Office of Sorsogon City (DepEd SDO). From the very beginning, we were welcomed with warmth and understanding. The staff guided us patiently, and we were even able to track the progress of our documents and approvals, which made the process less stressful.

On our first week, we were introduced to Ms. Natalia, the Admin Section's Administrative Officer V. She also oriented us about the different sections in the office, such as the Cashier, PSU, HR, Records, Budget, Accounting, and IT. After learning more about the various areas, she decided to deploy us to the IT Section, believing it was the most fitting place for us as BSIT students.

At the IT Section, we met Mr. Francis Lanuza, the Information Technology Officer of SDO Sorsogon City. He gave us an overview of the office operations, explaining the networks, systems, and digital processes they use to make work faster and more efficient. It was fascinating to see how IT plays such a crucial role in supporting the whole organization.

After the introductions, we were immediately guided on how to upload Division Memorandums, Advisories, and other important files to the DepEd SDO Sorsogon City website. Aside from handling these documents, part of our work also involved assisting people who came to the office looking for Mr. Lanuza. Most of their concerns revolved around Microsoft Office, password issues, or problems with their DepEd email. Since certain tasks, like user management, are reserved for permanent IT staff, our role was to help with other technical issues such as Wi-Fi connectivity, printer malfunctions, and basic computer troubleshooting.

We were also open to tasks from other departments, which sometimes involved sorting physical documents, digitizing hard copies, consolidating files, and assisting in small but meaningful ways. These experiences gave us the chance to learn not only about IT but also about administrative processes and office work in general.

What stood out to me the most this week was observing how the staff worked so diligently. Their dedication and teamwork inspired me to put more effort into my own tasks. I feel privileged to witness the hard work of the non-teaching personnel, who are just as essential in keeping the education system running smoothly.

Overall, my first week was both enlightening and inspiring. I look forward to being challenged further, gaining more knowledge, and contributing more in the coming weeks.
