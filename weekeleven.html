Teamwork in Action

Week Eleven

Reclassification continued to keep us busy this week—sorting documents, qualifying teachers, and even tracking down “missing” files.

On the second day of the week, after completing our usual tasks, we were allowed to go home earlier than usual in preparation for an incoming super typhoon. Before leaving, we ensured that important documents were safe and that devices such as computer setups, laptops, printers, and other equipment were covered with plastic to prevent damage from water.

Once the suspension was lifted, we returned to the office, rearranged everything back in order, and resumed work. Reclassification tasks continued to occupy the entire week. Through teamwork, we were even able to locate documents that were previously considered “missing.”

This week, I also met the ASDS’s new secretary, Sir Mark. I was once again tasked with training and guiding him, as he is a newly hired personnel appointed to serve in the ASDS Office. While assisting HR with reclassification, I briefed him on the types of tasks he would be expected to handle in the office.

During this reclassification season, I witnessed the incredible sense of collaboration in the office. Non-teaching staff from schools volunteered—or were asked by their school heads—to assist HR, non-HR staff helped out wherever needed, and other sections provided snacks as a gesture of appreciation for everyone’s hard work. This is what I truly admire about this office: the staff are not only welcoming but genuinely warm, making you feel like part of the team. Despite the challenges and tight deadlines, we were able to finish the first qualified batch on time.

I am beyond grateful to these amazing people who push us to work hard, face challenges, and yet still manage to make the experience enjoyable.
